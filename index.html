Financial Literacy Lexicon

1. Compound Interest: The Double-Edged Sword

Compounding calculates interest over previous accumulated interest. When you hold debt (like credit card lines or high student loans), high compounding interest acts as direct negative drag, trapping you in permanent budgeting cycles.

2. Assets vs. Liabilities

An Asset yields financial resources returning into your wallet (like index fund stocks, cash equity, etc.). A Liability drains resources out of your wallet on recurrent schedules (such as car finance debts). Build assets, not luxuries!

3. Opportunity Cost

The missed options of selecting one action path over another. For instance, splurging $1,000 on retail shopping yields fast short-term joy but deprives you of that money's compounding capability (~$3,000 in stocks over 15 years).

4. Biological Human Capital

Your organic health and skillset represents your most central early compound factor. Protecting personal physical vitality lowers unexpected medical penalties and retains base career focus bonuses!
