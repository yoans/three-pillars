Game Design Notes

LifeForge: The Three Pillars Sim

LifeForge is a turn-based financial literacy life sim about managing three connected pillars: health, wealth, and happiness. The goal is not to find one perfect answer. The goal is to understand tradeoffs, recover from mistakes, and see how small choices compound across a lifetime.

Health

Health represents physical energy and earning capacity. Low health can reduce income and trigger care costs. Gym, nutrition, rest, and preventive care protect future choices.

Wealth

Wealth is cash plus investments minus debt. Cash absorbs shocks, stocks compound slowly, and high-interest debt compounds against the player.

Happiness

Happiness captures joy, comfort, and mental strain. Experiences, relationships, and recovery can help it. Debt stress, overwork, and neglected health can drain it.

How The Game Plays

The game is turn-based. A life moment appears, the player reads the situation, compares choices, and picks one. After the decision is resolved, the player presses Next Season to advance time.

Each season is three months. The simulation applies income, expenses, debt interest, savings yield, stock growth, health drift, joy drift, habits, and any recovery effects. A new milestone, random event, or catch-up opportunity can appear after the season advances.

Choices are designed to teach tradeoffs rather than punish instantly. A good choice may cost money. A fun choice may create debt. A money-maximizing choice may damage health or joy. Recovery paths exist because financial literacy should include repair, not only warning signs.

Core Rules

  • Starting state: Age 18, $5,000 cash, no starting debt, 80 health, 75 happiness.
  • Cash shortfall: If costs push cash below $0, only the shortfall becomes credit-card debt at 19.8% APR.
  • Debt stress: Debt above $5,000 slightly drains joy; debt above $25,000 drains more joy.
  • Health income effect: Health over 85 adds a 5% focus bonus. Health below 30 cuts income by 15% and can trigger care costs.
  • Investing: Stock portfolio uses an 8% annual return assumption, applied seasonally.
  • Savings: Positive cash earns a small 2% annual yield.
  • 401(k): If active, 6% of salary goes into stocks and the employer match doubles that contribution.

Recurring Habit Effects

Gym Program

Costs $60/month. Adds +2 health and +1 joy each season.

Whole-Food Prep

Costs $300/month. Adds +1.5 health and +0.75 joy each season.

Weekend Outings

Costs $100/month. Adds +4 joy each season through social recovery.

Streaming Comfort

Costs $15/month. Adds +0.75 joy each season as a small comfort boost.

Full Readout

Scheduled Life Events

These milestone cards appear at specific ages. Each choice can change cash, debt, monthly costs, career state, housing, transportation, habits, health, happiness, and long-term compounding.

Age 18 - The Road Diverges Education

Choose the first path after school: prestige college, mechanical trade school, or immediate work. Teaches human capital, earning power, and loan tradeoffs.

  • Ivy League College: -$500 cash, +$30,000 debt, +5 health, +5 joy, student career, $300/month campus job.
  • Mechanical Trade School: -$1,000 cash, +$4,000 debt, -2 health, $800/month apprentice stipend.
  • Warehouse Loader: +$1,500 cash, no debt, -5 health, -2 joy, $1,800/month salary.

Age 19 - Selecting Where to Anchor Housing

Choose between low rent with discomfort or a more expensive studio with privacy and joy. Teaches the housing-cost floor.

  • House Share: $300/month rent, -4 health, -5 joy.
  • Studio Flat: $650/month rent, +2 health, +10 joy.

Age 20 - The Commuting Conundrum Transport

Compare public transit, a used car, and a financed sports sedan. Teaches depreciation, monthly drag, and status spending.

  • Transit Pass: $60/month transport, +2 health, -5 joy.
  • Beater Car: -$2,500 cash, $120/month transport, +12 joy.
  • Sports Sedan: -$500 cash, +$12,000 debt, $450/month transport plus $120/month insurance, +25 joy, temporary sports-car thrill.

Age 21 - Fuelling the Organic Vessel Nutrition

Food choices set recurring monthly cost and influence health. Teaches that cheap food can create later health and productivity costs.

  • Ramen and Microwaves: $150/month food, -12 health, -2 joy.
  • Balanced Home Cooking: $300/month food, +3 health, +5 joy.
  • Organic Farm Share: $600/month food, +15 health, +10 joy, activates the whole-food habit.

Age 22 - Entering Professional Career Paths Career

Move from school or early work into a career. Teaches salary compounding and lifestyle inflation risk.

  • Corporate Cadet Track: +$1,000 cash, -8 health, -4 joy, salary depends on education path: $3,800 for university, $2,400 for trade, $2,100 for warehouse promotion.
  • Creative Independent Designer: +4 health, +15 joy, freelance career, $2,000/month salary.

Age 24 - Trip Invitations: Vegas / Ibiza Calls Social

Choose between expensive memory-making, a cheaper alternative, or saving money. Teaches opportunity cost and credit-funded fun.

  • Charge It To Credit Cards: +$3,000 debt, -15 health, +25 joy.
  • Cheap Road Trip: -$400 cash, -2 health, +10 joy.
  • Stay Home: +6 health, -10 joy, preserves cash.

Age 26 - Gym Buddy Fitness Offer Wellness

Decide whether to pay for structured fitness or rely on free workouts. Teaches health as productive capital.

  • Gym Membership: $60/month gym cost, +20 health, +5 joy, activates gym habit.
  • Home Workouts: no monthly cost, +8 health.

Age 28 - Corporate 401(k) Match Opportunity Investment

Decide whether to contribute to an employer match. Teaches guaranteed match value and delayed gratification.

  • Contribute 6%: activates 401(k), employer match doubles contributions, -2 joy.
  • Skip Matching: keeps more spendable cash now, +5 joy, loses match benefit.

Age 30 - The Home Buying Milestone Lifestyle

Choose between buying a home or continuing to rent. Teaches equity, liquidity, and fixed housing cost.

  • Buy House: -$12,000 cash, $1,200/month mortgage, +6 health, +15 joy, adds $300/season home equity.
  • Keep Renting: preserves liquid capital, +2 joy.

Age 33 - Burnout & Overtime Hustle Work

Trade time and health for higher income, or protect balance. Teaches overwork as hidden debt.

  • Accept Promotion: salary increases 35%, -22 health, -12 joy.
  • Reject Overtime: +8 health, +8 joy, no salary increase.

Age 36 - Love & Marriage: The Big Wedding Plan Social

Compare a lavish financed wedding with a simple ceremony. Teaches debt pressure at household formation.

  • Fairy-Tale Wedding: -$4,000 cash, +$14,000 debt, -2 health, +30 joy.
  • Backyard Wedding: -$2,000 cash, +2 health, +20 joy, no new debt.

Age 40 - Stock Market Crash Opportunity Investment

Respond to a market crash. Teaches panic selling, discounted buying, and emotional discipline.

  • Buy The Discount: -$5,000 cash, +$7,500 portfolio value, +2 joy.
  • Hold Steady: no immediate stat changes.
  • Panic Liquidation: sells portfolio at a 30% loss, -4 health, -8 joy.

Age 44 - A Sudden Healthcare Challenge Health

Choose whether to pay for treatment or delay care. Teaches emergency funds and health outlays.

  • Full Medical Procedures: -$4,000 cash, +25 health, +8 joy.
  • Home Remedies: -$300 cash, -25 health, -15 joy.

Age 48 - Mid-Life Career Burnout Crisis Career

Choose between lower-stress work and continuing the grind. Teaches burnout, income, and self-care tradeoffs.

  • Part-Time Consulting: salary drops 30%, freelance career, +15 health, +18 joy.
  • Grind Through: keep income, -18 health, -10 joy.

Age 52 - Mid-Life Luxury Yacht / Corvette Temptation Lifestyle

Choose between status/luxury consumption and investing. Teaches the hedonic treadmill and recurring fees.

  • Acquire Luxury Machine: -$12,000 cash, +$150/month recurring cost, +20 joy.
  • Invest $12,000: +$12,000 portfolio value, +3 health, +2 joy.

Age 56 - A Distant Bequest Arrives Wealth

Allocate a $25,000 inheritance. Teaches windfall discipline and debt payoff.

  • Pay Debt And Invest Excess: uses up to $25,000 to reduce debt, invests any remaining amount.
  • World Cruise: effectively spends $15,000 and keeps $10,000 cash, +8 health, +30 joy.

Age 60 - Establishing Legacy Foundations Social

Choose a late-life legacy, health upgrade, or cash preservation. Teaches meaning, generosity, and retirement cushions.

  • Community Park: -$8,000 cash, +6 health, +26 joy.
  • Spa Deck: -$8,000 cash, +14 health, +14 joy.
  • Preserve Cash: no cash cost, -5 joy.

Between Milestones

Random Seasonal Events

Major Dental Extraction Pain Health

Emergency dental treatment tests whether cash is available for a surprise health cost.

  • Surgery: -$1,200 cash, +10 health, +4 joy.
  • Refuse Treatment: -15 health, -10 joy.

Streaming Subscriptions Overload Spending

Subscription creep tests whether small recurring costs are being audited.

  • Cancel Subscriptions: reduces subscription cost by up to $100/month, -4 joy.
  • Retain Memberships: +$30/month subscription cost, -1 health, +4 joy.

Speculative Crypto / Meme Coin FOMO Investment

Speculative investing tests risk appetite and teaches the difference between investing and gambling.

  • Decline Gamble: +1 health.
  • Speculate: -$1,500 cash, -4 health, +5 joy. 20% chance to add $8,000 portfolio value; otherwise the stake is lost.

HVAC Climate / Auto Breakdowns Lifestyle

A surprise repair tests the emergency fund.

  • Pay Repairs: -$1,500 cash, +2 joy.
  • Delay Fixes: -8 health, -12 joy.

Recovery Systems

Catch-Up Opportunities

These cards are conditional. They appear when the player is in trouble or when the model detects a teaching moment. The design goal is to show realistic repair paths, not only consequences.

Recovery Plan Check-In Health below 35

A health coach offers recovery before collapse.

  • Gym and Sleep Routine: activates gym, +18 health, +6 joy.
  • Community Clinic: -$500 cash, +12 health, +2 joy.
  • Reset Week: -$300 cash, +8 health, +8 joy.

Credit Counselor Catch-Up Plan High-interest debt

A nonprofit counselor offers realistic debt stabilization.

  • Hardship Plan: waives up to 18% of debt, capped at $9,000, lowers APR to 9%.
  • Sell Lifestyle And Refinance: reduces debt up to 22%, capped at $12,000, lowers APR to 11%, may remove sports-car costs.
  • Keep Current Plan: -3 joy, no immediate financial change.

Cash And Debt Strategy Check Cash with debt

A coach appears when cash could reduce debt.

  • Pay Debt From Cash: pays a focused chunk while keeping reserves, +5 joy.
  • Keep Cash Buffer: +1 joy, interest remains.
  • Refinance First: -$300 cash, lowers APR to 8.5%, +3 joy.
